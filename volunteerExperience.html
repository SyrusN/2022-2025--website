Performing Maintenance on Computer Networks on the Island of Antigua [February 2020]

One of the best opportunities I've had so far was when I went to the island of Antigua to set up and perform maintenance on the computer networks there. In doing so, me and my group of four other students went around to various primary schools to replace the rusting computers, deteriorating Cat5e/Cat6 cables, and older Cisco switches. The reason the existing equipment rusted was due to the island's air current that washes over the island like a cool breeze. However, this breeze carries moisture from the ocean, thus provided ample conditions for rusting.

For the rusting computers, we went ahead and replaced them with laptops for when mobility or computer performance was an issue. For the cables, we found that some of the dangling cables exposed to the elements of weather, or to primary school students were prone to damage. To address this, my group decided to replace these long-running cables with Ubiquiti point-to-point bridges (P2Ps). In other situations, we decided to restrain newly installed cables with zip ties to pipes and other school infrastructure. For the old network switches, we found that either they were no longer operating to their full capacity, or just not working. Thus, we replaced these with new switches. These switches were mostly Cisco Power over Ethernet (PoE) switches that we needed for powering the Ubiquiti P2Ps and Cisco access points. Besides performing maintenance on the existing computer networks, we also installed Cisco access points that were preconfigured before the trip by another Cisco Networking Academy student. We used these for the laptops being installed since the students needed mobility when doing class work.

Overall, the trip was fun since it gave me the opportunity to get hands-on experience with some basic remote network technician work.

2025 Syrus Nelson
